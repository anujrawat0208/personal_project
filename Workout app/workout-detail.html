About This Workout

This full body strength workout is designed for beginners looking to build strength and muscle. The routine focuses on the major muscle groups and uses compound movements to maximize efficiency. All exercises can be done with minimal equipment, making it perfect for home workouts or at the gym.

Complete 3 sets of each exercise with a 60-second rest between sets. If you're just starting out, focus on proper form rather than the number of repetitions. As you get stronger, you can increase the weight or resistance to continue challenging your muscles.

Exercises

  • Squats
    1
    3 sets
    10 reps
    60 sec rest
    Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your body as if sitting in a chair, keeping chest up and knees over toes. Return to starting position.
  • Push-ups
    2
    3 sets
    8 reps
    60 sec rest
    Begin in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulders. Lower your body until chest nearly touches the floor, then push back up. Modify by doing push-ups on knees if needed.
  • Dumbbell Rows
    3
    3 sets
    10 reps each side
    60 sec rest
    With a dumbbell in one hand, place opposite knee and hand on a bench. Keep back flat and pull the dumbbell up to your side, then lower back down. Complete all reps on one side before switching.
  • Lunges
    4
    3 sets
    10 reps each leg
    60 sec rest
    Step forward with one leg and lower your body until both knees are at 90-degree angles. Push back to starting position and repeat with the other leg. Alternate legs for the required repetitions.
  • Plank
    5
    3 sets
    30 seconds
    60 sec rest
    Hold a push-up position with body in a straight line from head to heels. Engage your core and hold the position for the specified time. Focus on breathing and maintaining proper form.

Equipment Needed

  • Dumbbells (light to moderate weight)
  • Exercise mat
  • Bench or sturdy chair
  • Water bottle

Tips & Notes

  • Warm up for 5-10 minutes before starting.
  • Focus on form over the number of repetitions.
  • Breathe out during the exertion phase of each exercise.
  • Stretch after completing the workout.
  • Stay hydrated throughout your session.
